# @damatjs/provider-payment 1.0.0

`PaymentProviderService(config)` now extends `ModuleService` and requires the
production payment lifecycle: create/get/list, capture, cancellation, refund
creation/lookup, and raw-body webhook verification. Applications may also use
an ordinary structurally compatible module service.

Amounts use integer minor units, remote mutations carry idempotency keys, and
refunds have a distinct `RefundRecord`. Runtime record guards and a concrete
normal-module fixture cover valid and malformed provider results.

Payment modules retain direct access to their credentials, models,
transactions, caching, and events. The framework does not interpret payment
operations or own provider-specific routes and lifecycle.
