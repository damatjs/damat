# @damatjs/workflow-engine 0.6.0

> Safer defaults for retries and locking: steps are presumed idempotent (and
> non-idempotent steps are never retried), locks auto-extend by default, and
> lock-backend failures surface as structured results instead of throws.

## What changed

### Idempotency drives retries

- `DEFAULT_STEP_CONFIG.idempotent` flips `false` → `true`: steps are presumed
  safe to retry.
- When a step sets `idempotent: false`, its retry policy is **ignored** — the
  first failure goes straight to the compensation path rather than re-invoking a
  side-effecting step. A warning is logged if a retry policy is configured on a
  non-idempotent step.

### Lock auto-extend on by default

`DEFAULT_AUTO_EXTEND` is now `true`: executions longer than the lock TTL keep
their mutual exclusion via heartbeat. Set `autoExtend: false` only if you want a
hung-but-alive runner's lock to lapse for takeover — crashed processes stop
heartbeating on their own.

### Lock failures are structured

- `executeWithLock` catches lock **acquisition** errors and returns a
  `WorkflowResult` with `LOCK_BACKEND_UNAVAILABLE` instead of throwing.
- Lock **release** errors are logged but never mask the workflow result; the
  lock still expires via TTL.

## Upgrade notes

- Steps with side effects that are not safe to repeat must declare
  `idempotent: false` — under the new default they would otherwise be retried.
- If you relied on locks lapsing at TTL during long executions, pass
  `autoExtend: false` explicitly.
